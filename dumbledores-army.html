Dumbledore's Army: The Secret Student Militia Operating Beneath Hogwarts — Exposed

A clandestine organisation of some thirty Hogwarts students, meeting in the castle's Room of Requirement under the personal instruction of Harry Potter, has been uncovered by High Inquisitor Umbridge following a tip from a student informant. Members have been practising advanced offensive and defensive spells in defiance of Educational Decree Twenty-Four. Albus Dumbledore — whose name the group adopted as both tribute and provocation — has fled the castle rather than face Ministry charges.
By Special Report · High Inquisitor's Office & Ministry Correspondent

High Inquisitor Dolores Umbridge moved swiftly and decisively in the early hours of Thursday morning, following intelligence provided to her office by a student informant whose identity is being withheld by the Ministry at their own request. Acting on that intelligence, Umbridge confronted Harry Potter — fifth-year Gryffindor student, Hogwarts Quidditch Seeker, and, it now emerges, the leader and primary instructor of an unsanctioned student Defence organisation — and within the hour had assembled evidence of the group's activities, its membership list, and confirmation that the organisation had been meeting in the school's legendary Room of Requirement since at least October of last year.

The organisation, it has emerged, operated under the name "Dumbledore's Army." The name was not, it appears, a coincidence. It was chosen, according to the signed membership parchment recovered by Umbridge and now in Ministry possession, explicitly in reference to the school's headmaster — in part, sources suggest, as a gesture of support for Dumbledore's much-disputed claims about Voldemort's return, and in part, rather more provocatively, as a deliberate two-word response to the Ministry's attempts to characterise those claims as dangerous fantasy.

That Ministry officials found this amusing is not among the things we are in a position to report.

The membership list, which runs to approximately thirty names drawn from all four Hogwarts houses — a detail that has surprised several observers given the school's traditional inter-house tensions — includes some of the school's most academically distinguished students. Hermione Granger, regarded by staff as among the outstanding students of her generation. Neville Longbottom, grandson of the well-known pure-blood Longbottom family. Ginny Weasley, youngest child of Arthur and Molly Weasley of Ottery St Catchpole. Luna Lovegood, daughter of Xenophilius Lovegood, editor of the Quibbler. And, at the head of the list: Harry James Potter, whose capacity for attracting the sort of institutional attention that careers are ended by has, over five years at Hogwarts, shown no signs of diminishing.

What They Were Doing — And Why It Was Illegal

Educational Decree Number Twenty-Four, issued by the Ministry in October 1995, prohibits the formation of any student organisation, club, society, team, or group without the express approval of the High Inquisitor. The decree was issued following an earlier confrontation between Umbridge and several students and was understood, at the time of its announcement, to be primarily targeted at Hogwarts' Quidditch teams, which had been briefly suspended following a brawl involving several Gryffindor players.

Dumbledore's Army did not apply for High Inquisitor approval. This is, on reflection, not surprising given that the organisation's primary purpose was to provide students with the practical Defence training that Umbridge's own classes had ceased to offer — a fact that lends the situation a circularity that the Ministry's statement has not directly addressed.

According to witness accounts provided to the High Inquisitor's office, sessions of Dumbledore's Army involved the practical instruction and practice of a range of spells well in advance of standard Hogwarts curriculum for the participating year groups. These included the Patronus Charm — an advanced piece of magic that many qualified adult witches and wizards cannot produce — as well as Stunning spells, Shield charms, Disarming jinxes, and, reportedly, early work on what a member described only as "more advanced things." Potter, who has demonstrated abilities in Defence that several of his teachers have described as exceptional, appears to have been a genuinely capable instructor. The Ministry considers this aggravating rather than mitigating. The students who attended the sessions are unlikely to agree.

"They called it Dumbledore's Army. Thirty students from five houses, learning to defend themselves, led by a fifteen-year-old boy. One might ask what drove them to it. The answer is not particularly comfortable reading for the Ministry."

Dumbledore's Flight — The Headmaster Departs

The morning's most dramatic development came not from Potter's confrontation with Umbridge but from what followed it. When Cornelius Fudge arrived at Hogwarts with two Aurors and a representative of the Wizengamot to arrest Dumbledore on charges of conspiracy to form an illegal organisation and to train students for what the Minister described as "insurrectionary purposes," Dumbledore was gone.

Not apprehended, not restrained, not talked down. Gone. He left, according to witnesses including Umbridge, Fudge, and several students who happened to be in the corridor, by simply announcing that he did not intend to go to Azkaban and that he had "no desire to be Stunned by two Aurors whose capability he did not wish to insult but whose prospects of succeeding he considered remote." He then — and this is the detail that the Ministry has found most difficult to digest — Transfigured two staff members into immobility, incapacitated the Aurors with a single contained spell, and departed through the school's front doors in a manner witnesses describe as "almost leisurely."

Fudge, by multiple accounts, was briefly unable to speak. He recovered sufficiently to announce that Dumbledore was henceforth dismissed from his positions as Hogwarts headmaster, Chief Warlock of the Wizengamot, and Supreme Mugwump of the International Confederation of Wizards. He then appointed Umbridge as Acting Headmistress, a decision which the Hogwarts school portraits — who have considerable institutional memory and correspondingly strong views — apparently received with noises this reporter's source declined to reproduce in print.

Dumbledore's whereabouts are, at time of press, unknown. The Ministry has issued a warrant for his arrest. Several senior figures in the wizarding world have privately contacted this newspaper to express the view that Cornelius Fudge may have just made the most significant political error of his tenure, and that the question is not whether this will become clear, but when.

Potter Punished — The Ministry's Response to the Students

Harry Potter has been suspended from the Hogwarts Quidditch team for the remainder of the season and placed on academic probation pending a Ministry review of his conduct. He has not been expelled — a decision that surprised several Ministry observers — and the broader membership of Dumbledore's Army has received detentions rather than suspension. Umbridge, who will deal personally with the disciplinary process in her new capacity as Acting Headmistress, has not specified publicly what those detentions will involve. Students who have previously served detention with the High Inquisitor have been, this reporter notes, unusually reluctant to discuss the experience.

Potter made a brief statement to classmates that has been reported to us by three separate sources as including the words "he's not gone for good" in reference to Dumbledore, delivered with what was described as a confidence at some distance from the expression of a student who has just seen his headmaster flee the school and his own Quidditch career temporarily ended. Whether that confidence is well-founded, time will tell. If the past five years of Harry Potter's life at Hogwarts are any guide, however, the sensible money is not on the Ministry.

⚠ Ministry Advisory — Educational Decree Update
Following this morning's events, the Ministry of Magic advises all Hogwarts students and their families of the following:

Educational Decree Number Twenty-Four remains in full force. All student organisations, of any kind, remain prohibited without High Inquisitor approval.

Educational Decree Number Twenty-Eight has been issued this morning, appointing Dolores Umbridge as Acting Headmistress of Hogwarts with immediate effect.

Educational Decree Number Twenty-Nine has been issued simultaneously, granting the Acting Headmistress authority over all disciplinary matters at the school without requirement to consult the Deputy Headmistress or any other member of staff.

Albus Dumbledore is no longer associated with Hogwarts in any capacity. Parents or guardians with concerns about the transition of leadership should contact the Ministry's Educational Standards Office.
🟢 Leader · The Fifteen-Year-Old Instructor
Harry James Potter
Age 15, Gryffindor fifth year. Suspended from Quidditch. On academic probation. Has been producing a Patronus since his third year, survived the killing curse at age one, and competed in the Triwizard Tournament at fourteen. The Ministry would very much like Harry Potter to be a confused, attention-seeking, unreliable witness. Harry Potter appears to be something else entirely.
⚡ Namesake · Fled the School
Albus Percival Wulfric Brian Dumbledore
Formerly Headmaster of Hogwarts, Chief Warlock of the Wizengamot, Supreme Mugwump of the International Confederation of Wizards. Now: a fugitive from a Ministry warrant, divested of his titles, absent from the school he has led for thirty-seven years. And, it would appear, entirely unconcerned about any of it. A Ministry warrant has been issued. The Ministry would do well to consider the historical success rate of attempts to restrain Albus Dumbledore against his will before investing too heavily in its optimism.
