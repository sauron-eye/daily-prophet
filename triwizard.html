The Greatest Magical Contest in History Returns: Hogwarts to Host the Triwizard Tournament for the First Time in One Hundred and Four Years

Dumbledore announces the grand revival of the ancient three-school competition, last held in 1890 and discontinued after a series of fatalities. Champions from Hogwarts, Beauxbatons Academy of Magic, and the Durmstrang Institute will compete for the Triwizard Cup and a prize of one thousand Galleons. An Age Line will prevent underage entry. Or so the Ministry hopes.
By Bartholomew Phelps, Education & Sport Correspondent · Hogwarts Castle, Scotland

The announcement came at the Hogwarts start-of-term feast on the first of September, delivered by Albus Dumbledore in the Great Hall to an audience of some nine hundred students and staff who, by all accounts, received the news with the sort of thunderous collective enthusiasm not heard in the castle since the announcement that Nicholas de Mimsy-Porpington would be throwing his five-hundredth Deathday Party.

The Triwizard Tournament — the grandest, most celebrated, and most historically lethal inter-school magical competition the wizarding world has ever produced — is to be revived. This autumn, for the first time in one hundred and four years, the great entrance hall of Hogwarts will welcome delegations from Beauxbatons Academy of Magic in southern France and from the Durmstrang Institute, whose location remains classified. A single champion will be selected from each school, and those three champions will compete across three tasks designed to test "magical prowess, intelligence, and courage" — or, to be blunter about it, to push their participants to and occasionally beyond the limits of what the human body and mind can withstand.

It is worth being clear-eyed about this history. The Tournament was discontinued in 1890 not as a matter of routine scheduling, nor because interest had waned, but because the cumulative death toll among its competitors had reached a point at which the sponsoring schools could no longer, with any credibility, describe the event as a competition rather than an ordeal. Three deaths in the final edition alone. The Ministry of Magic at the time issued a strongly worded recommendation that the Tournament be suspended indefinitely. "Indefinitely," it turns out, meant until Albus Dumbledore decided otherwise.

Dumbledore, at the feast, acknowledged this history with the measured candour that distinguishes him from most of his contemporaries in positions of authority. "The Tournament has not been without its dangers in the past," he told the assembled students. "However, we have taken extensive precautions this year, working closely with the Ministry of Magic, to ensure the safety of all participants." He then announced that an Age Line would be drawn around the Goblet of Fire — the enchanted selection mechanism — to prevent any student below the age of seventeen from submitting their name for consideration. The students received this news with what reporters at the scene described as a mixture of disappointment and barely concealed scheming.

The Three Schools — What We Know

Beauxbatons Academy of Magic is the premier wizarding school of France, Spain, and much of southern Europe. It is directed by Olympe Maxime, a witch of considerable physical stature and formidable reputation, who is understood to have travelled personally to negotiate the terms of Beauxbatons' participation. The school's students are, by tradition, selected for their elegance and precision in spellwork rather than the more confrontational approach favoured at Durmstrang. Their delegation is expected to arrive at Hogwarts in October.

The Durmstrang Institute, by contrast, is a school whose reputation precedes it in ways that are not entirely comfortable. Situated in the far northern reaches of Europe — its precise location is among the most jealously guarded secrets in international wizardry — Durmstrang is known for its rigorous approach to the Dark Arts, which it teaches as a core subject rather than the defensive elective it represents at Hogwarts. Its current Headmaster, Igor Karkaroff, is a name that will be familiar to readers with long memories: he was tried before the Wizengamot in 1981 as a suspected Death Eater, and secured his release by providing information leading to the arrest of several of his former associates. His appointment to the Durmstrang headmastership three years later was controversial. It remains so.

Among Durmstrang's student body this year is Viktor Krum, the Bulgarian international Quidditch Seeker whose performance in the World Cup final last month — an extraordinary display even in defeat — made him arguably the most famous seventeen-year-old in the wizarding world. His presence at the Tournament has already generated considerable interest, and this newspaper anticipates considerable coverage.

The Tasks — What History Tells Us

The Ministry has declined to provide advance information on this year's specific tasks, citing concerns about fairness to the competing schools. What history tells us is that previous Tournaments have included challenges involving dragons, water creatures, and enchanted mazes of sufficient complexity to disorient the most capable adult wizard. The 1792 Tournament featured a task involving the capture of a Cockatrice that escaped its enclosure and injured all three judges. The 1890 edition's fatal final task was never publicly described in its entirety. We note all of this not to alarm, but because our readers deserve to understand what "extensive precautions" are being precautioned against.

What has been confirmed is that the selection process will be managed by the Goblet of Fire — an ancient artefact of considerable magical power that will assess applications and select the most worthy candidate from each school. The Goblet will be placed in the Entrance Hall of Hogwarts from the twenty-ninth of October. Students wishing to be considered must submit their name on a piece of parchment. The Age Line, drawn by Dumbledore himself, will prevent submission by any student under seventeen years old.

This newspaper notes that the Age Line is Dumbledore's solution to a problem that is, at root, a problem of desire and ambition — two qualities that have never, in Hogwarts' thousand-year history, been reliably deterred by lines drawn on floors.

"The Triwizard Tournament has not been revived because it is safe. It has been revived because it is magnificent. Those two things are not always compatible, and the Ministry would do well to remember it."

— Bartholomew Phelps, Daily Prophet Education Correspondent
Hogwarts Students React — Excitement, Ambition, and One Unfortunate Set of Twins

The news was received at Hogwarts with an enthusiasm that suggests the historical mortality statistics are either unknown to the current student body or known and considered, in the way of the young, to be someone else's problem. By the morning of the second of September, the entrance hall notice boards were already plastered with informal discussion parchments, training schedules, and at least three separate advertisements from students offering "pre-Tournament coaching" at rates this reporter considers optimistic given their ages.

The most significant early response came from the Hogwarts Gryffindor contingent, where sources indicate that the Weasley twins — Fred and George, sixth-year students widely known for their entrepreneurial approach to the school's regulations — have already announced their intention to circumvent the Age Line using what they describe as an Ageing Potion. Their logic, when this reporter pressed them on it during a brief corridor conversation, was that since the Age Line presumably tests apparent age rather than actual age, a convincingly brewed Ageing Potion should do the trick. This reporter declines to speculate on whether they are correct, but notes that Dumbledore has been setting enchantments since before their grandfather was born and may have considered the obvious workarounds.

Among the older students eligible to enter, the mood is rather more considered. Cedric Diggory, the Hufflepuff sixth-year who captains the Hufflepuff Quidditch team and is widely regarded as one of the school's outstanding all-round wizards, has confirmed to classmates that he intends to submit his name. Percy Weasley, now safely installed in his new Ministry position, has written from London expressing the view that the whole enterprise is "extremely exciting and thoroughly well-organised, which reflects enormously well on the Ministry." The Tournament organisers will be glad to know it.

📜 The Triwizard Tournament — Key Facts
Founded: Medieval period — exact date disputed. Certainly pre-dates the International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy (1689).
Last held: 1890, at Hogwarts. Discontinued following three fatalities in the final task.
Participating schools: Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry (Scotland); Beauxbatons Academy of Magic (France); The Durmstrang Institute (Northern Europe, location classified).
Selection: The Goblet of Fire chooses one champion per school from submitted candidates. The Goblet's decision is considered magically binding — the selected champion must compete.
Prize: The Triwizard Cup and one thousand Galleons.
Age restriction: Minimum age seventeen. Age Line to be enforced by Albus Dumbledore personally.
Tasks: Three. Details classified. Historically have involved dangerous creatures, complex enchantments, and extended duration under extreme conditions.
🏆 Tournament Host · Headmaster
Albus Percival Wulfric Brian Dumbledore
Order of Merlin, First Class. Grand Sorcerer. Chief Warlock of the Wizengamot. Supreme Mugwump of the International Confederation of Wizards. Headmaster of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. The man who has chosen to bring the Triwizard Tournament back from its hundred-year suspension. Also the man who, presumably, is confident that this time it will go smoothly.
🌟 Durmstrang · International Star
Viktor Krum
Age 18. Bulgarian international Seeker. Widely considered the finest Seeker of his generation. Caught the Golden Snitch in the World Cup Final despite Bulgaria's defeat, in one of the most celebrated individual Quidditch moments in living memory. Now at Hogwarts as a Durmstrang student competitor. The Tournament's unofficial favourite before a single task has begun.
Let the Tournament Begin — and May the Best Witch or Wizard Prevail.
